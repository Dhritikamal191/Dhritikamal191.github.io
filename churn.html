Predictive Modeling and Financial Risk Scoring for Bank Customer Churn

An end-to-end machine learning and business analytics platform designed to predict customer churn, quantify financial risk exposure, and optimize retention strategies for banking institutions.

Business Problem

Customer attrition directly impacts profitability in the banking sector. Acquiring a new customer is significantly more expensive than retaining an existing one. The objective of this project was to identify customers at risk of churn and transform predictive insights into actionable business decisions.

Project Objectives

  • Predict customer churn probability using machine learning.
  • Identify key factors driving customer attrition.
  • Estimate potential financial loss due to churn.
  • Simulate retention strategies and measure ROI.
  • Monitor model performance and data drift.

Dataset Overview

The project utilized the Bank Customer Churn dataset containing demographic, financial, and behavioral information for 10,000 customers.

Data Challenges

Class Imbalance

Churned customers represented a minority class, leading to biased model performance. To address this challenge, SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Oversampling Technique) was incorporated into the training pipeline.

Feature Preprocessing

Numerical features were standardized while categorical features were encoded using a preprocessing pipeline to ensure consistency across all models.

Machine Learning Pipeline

Raw Data → Preprocessing → Scaling → SMOTE → Model Training → Risk Scoring

The entire workflow was automated using Scikit-Learn and Imbalanced-Learn pipelines to prevent data leakage and maintain reproducibility.

Financial Risk Modeling

Beyond churn prediction, the platform estimates financial exposure by calculating customer value and expected churn loss.

Customer Value = 0.5 × Balance + 0.3 × Estimated Salary + 10,000 × Number of Products
Expected Churn Loss = Churn Probability × Customer Value

Retention Strategy Simulation

An interactive scenario analysis engine was developed to evaluate retention campaigns and estimate business outcomes.

  • A/B Testing Simulation
  • Retention Cost Estimation
  • Expected Savings Analysis
  • Return on Investment (ROI)
ROI = (Expected Savings − Retention Cost) / Retention Cost
